IOWA LAKES COMMUNITY COLLEGE STUDENTS PLACE AT
NATIONAL CONFERENCE AND WIN PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION

Iowa Lakes students participating in a national competition came home this week with numerous honors. Eleven students placed in the top 10 nationally in the Business Professionals of America Leadership Conference held in Philadelphia, Penn., April 23-27.

In addition, Iowa Lakes is the home college of the new national president of the organization. Carolann Fullerton, Peterson, an Accounting Specialist student from the chapter located at the Spencer Center, will serve as the national president of BPA. During her year as president, Fullerton will lead the Business Professionals of America which networks education, business and industry, and contributes to the preparation of a world-class workforce through the advancement of leadership, citizenship, academic and technological skills.

Fullerton's home chapter, the Iowa Lakes Spencer Center BPA, also received another distinguished honor. The chapter won the "Banner Award." The Banner Award is given to only one chapter each year and is selected on the basis of club activity in five separate areas including business knowledge, financial, civic, social and promotional activities.

Over 4,000 delegates from throughout the United States participated in Business Professional of America's 33rd National Leadership Conference. Activities included general sessions with keynote speakers, business meetings, leadership workshops, occupational and specialized competitive events, election of national officers and tours of Philadelphia area sites. The highlight of the conference was the presentation of the awards to the winners in the national competitive events.

Professors accompanying the students to the national competition included Lecia Berven, Accounting Specialist Instructor; Deb Jones, Office Technology Professor; and, Becky Simons, Assistant Professor of Business.

Spencer Chapter Awards:
"Banner Award" - This award is given to one chapter a year and to earn this award a club must be active in five areas.
First: The members must participate in professional activities that will promote and increase their business knowledge and skills. This area requires at least 5 activities.
The second area of the award is financial activities. Five are required in this area as well. Such as tutoring accounting, aiding individuals with checkbooks accounting or taxes, or preparing a budget.
The third area of the award is based on the clubs civic activities. Five activities are required in this area as well. Disaster relief, coats for kids, Big Sisters group, Special Olympics, etc.
The fourth area of the award is based on the clubs social activites. Five activities are required in this area as well such as get together with other clubs, Halloween party for children, etc.
The fifth area of the award is based on the clubs promotional activities for Business Professionals of America such as involving businesses at meetings, brochure production to explain the organization, or promoting BPAs during National Business Professionals Week, etc. Five activities in this area are also required.

"Special Award" The Spencer Chapter also received recognition for its support of Special Olympics which is supported nationally as the Business Professionals chosen charity.

"Membership Recognition" The Spencer Chapter also received recognition for its membership explosion, which has doubled this year.

National President Business Professionals of America President. Carolann Fullerton will serve the United States for the 1999 to 2000 year. This position will provide her with an excellent opportunity for growth personally and educationally. We are extremely proud of her accomplishment.
