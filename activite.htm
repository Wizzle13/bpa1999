October
The Student Senate sponsored a pumpkin carving contest in which any organization or club in the college could participate. As Business Professionals we decided this would be a good way to raise a little money and have fun doing it. Beth, Stephanie, Kim, Chris Holl, and Gena put forth the effect and carved themselves a great pumpkin . We found it to be a great diversion from the pressures of schoolwork and a chance to grow by expressing our creativity. Our pumpkin won third place and we received $25 for our effort.
The Aviation club along with the Red Cross sponsored a blood drive at our school. We felt that it was a way to give directly of ourselves and help people that we didn't know. We improve ourselves by helping others. Mike, Kim, Stephanie, and Lecia our advisor assisted in the drive by giving blood and helping to sign up other participants.
The Fall Leadership Conference was held in Des Moines, Iowa on October 21 and 22. Kim, Beth, Gena, Jay, and Mike participated in the conference. We all had a really good time and got to know everyone better.

November
Our club sponsored a winter clothing drive. We put up fliers and set out boxes throughout the college. The response was tremendous: we collected boxes and bags of winter clothing to donate to the needy. The final total was two large boxes and eight to ten large plastic bags of clothing. We help ourselves to grow by improving the lives of others.

December
Once again the Student Senate sponsored a hall decoration contest. Another fun time was about to begin. Our creativity came out and we were able to cheer people up and get them into the spirit of Christmas. Jolene came up with our theme, Count down to Christmas, and Beth and Gena decorated a tree with cut out numbers and strips of calculator tape as garland. Chris Burton use his computer to make the banner stating our theme. We received many comments about our decoration, but did not win an award from the Student Senate. The great complements from the passersby were fantastic and made the effort well worth it.
Our club decided to sponsor a needy family for Christmas. We gain by helping others in need. The staff at Upper Des Moines Opportunity chose a family for us, and Stephanie and Beth went on a shopping trip. We allocated $100 toward the project. The information sheet we received gave us super ideas and the sizes for each member of the family. Our club also decided to purchase food for a wonderful Christmas dinner for the family.
Our club held our Christmas party at the school for everyone to participate. Treats were brought by all, and enjoyed by all . We sat around telling stories and laughing . It was a good party.

January
Time for raising more money. The college held a Jazz Band competition which usually brings 1,000 extra people into the college. We decided to hold a bake sale. We would feed their bodies while the competition would challenge their minds and spirits. Everyone picked an item to bake and we purchased homemade rolls from the bakery. Our response to the bake sale was great; those high school kids could sure eat. We raised $100 after expenses.
We have sought to improve the world around us; sometimes through sacrifice, sometimes not. We have gained a measure of excellence as we follow this path and have grown from the experience.
